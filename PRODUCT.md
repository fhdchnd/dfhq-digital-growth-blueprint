# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The DFHQ recruiter and hiring manager for the Digital Growth and Programs Manager role. They screen a high volume of applications, often scanning on a phone between calls or meetings. They are not technical, but they know their own job description cold and will notice if a candidate actually engaged with it versus sent generic filler.

## Product Purpose

A single-page pitch that replaces a cover letter. It has one job: convince the reader in under a minute that Fahad Chandio already thinks and operates like the person this role requires, then get him a call. Success is a reply, not a page view.

## Brand Personality

Direct. Opinionated. Seasoned operator, not a job applicant performing enthusiasm. Reads like a real strategy memo from someone who has actually shipped things, not a marketing site selling a service. Confident without posturing, specific without showing off.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS landing page templates. Gradient hero sections. Stock "hire me" personal-brand sites with a big centered photo and buzzword pills. Corporate pitch-deck aesthetics (navy and gold, faux-premium). Anything that looks assembled from a component library rather than written by one person who means every line.

## Design Principles

- Specificity is the entire pitch. Every claim ties to a real number or a real system Fahad built, never a vague competency.
- Typography and content carry the authority. No decoration standing in for substance.
- One clear point of view per section, stated plainly, not hedged.
- Restraint reads as seniority. The page should look like it took confidence to make this simple, not budget to make it fancy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text at 4.5:1 contrast minimum. Full motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. No content or navigation gated behind hover-only or motion-only interaction.
