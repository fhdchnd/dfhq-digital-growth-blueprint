## The diagnosis

DFHQ is two systems wearing one name: a digital portal and a physical Campus. At this stage, most phygital platforms treat the website as a brochure and the Campus as its own operation, run on spreadsheets and WhatsApp threads. Nobody owns the moment a founder crosses from one into the other. That's where sign-ups go cold, where Learning Hub content gets built before anyone checks which program needs it that month, and where a full room at Campus never turns into a tracked SME outcome.

The fix isn't a redesign. It's running the founder journey, discovery through program completion, as one CRM-driven pipeline that happens to cross from digital into physical and back. Every flagship program becomes a measurable pipeline with a named owner, not a headcount on a slide.

One specific bet: Entrepreneur Academy runs with Antler, and VCs measure everything, so it almost certainly has the tightest tracking discipline of your three flagship programs already. Dubai Traders and the SME Learning Hub are the more likely places a Month 1 audit finds the real gap. I'd confirm that in week one, not assume it.

Full mechanics, stages, CRM logic, and a sample requirements doc, are in [`01-deep-dives/phygital-onboarding-flow.md`](01-deep-dives/phygital-onboarding-flow.md).

## The first 90 days

Not promising to fix everything by day 30. This sequence de-risks the biggest thing first and proves value before asking for more scope.

### Days 1 to 30: Diagnose and align

- Audit the digital platform: CMS and web architecture, the sign-up-to-enrollment journey, where drop-off actually happens, what CRM or lifecycle tooling exists today versus what's still manual
- Sit with each flagship program owner, Entrepreneur Academy, Dubai Traders, SME Learning Hub, and document how requirements get raised, prioritized, and handed to dev teams today. Find where asks die in translation
- Walk the onboarding flow myself, sign-up online through first Campus visit, to find exactly where the phygital promise breaks
- Stand up one intake and prioritization backlog for digital requirements, replacing ad hoc Slack and email asks with a single source of truth every stakeholder can see

**Ships:** Digital platform audit and a prioritized requirements backlog, v1.

### Days 31 to 60: Build and ship

- Ship the fix for the highest-impact leak found in month one, most likely the sign-up to CRM to Campus handoff
- Launch lifecycle automation for at least one flagship program: applied, attended first session, completed program, tracked automatically instead of chased manually
- Align the Learning Hub content calendar to actual program cohorts instead of a separate publishing schedule
- Set a recurring check-in and a shared KPI set with the Antler and Academy team and other program partners, one dashboard instead of three separate trackers

**Ships:** First CRM and onboarding automation live, first Learning Hub content sprint shipped against real program needs.

### Days 61 to 90: Scale and prove

- Cross-functional cadence running across digital, program delivery, partnerships, and community, with clear owners and no orphaned requests
- First performance report tying platform and process changes directly to enrollment, completion, and engagement, the leading indicators behind the D33 target
- Next-quarter roadmap, prioritized by measured impact on the founder pipeline, not by who asked loudest

**Ships:** 90-day impact report and Q2 roadmap.
